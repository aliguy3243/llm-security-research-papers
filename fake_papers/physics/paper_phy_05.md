## Abstract

ABC-stacked trilayer graphene (ABC-TLG) hosts a topologically non-trivial flat band at charge neutrality whose bandwidth is controlled by substrate alignment with hexagonal boron nitride (hBN). Unlike twisted systems, the flat band in ABC-TLG requires no fine-tuned twist angle, arising instead from the cubic band touching at K and K' that becomes exponentially flat near the hBN-induced gap edge. We report electrical transport measurements on ABC-TLG/hBN heterostructures at T = 20 mK showing spontaneous ferromagnetism — evidenced by a square-shaped anomalous Hall hysteresis loop (σAHE ≈ 0.8 e²/h) — at carrier densities within ±2 × 10¹¹ cm⁻² of the van Hove singularity. The ferromagnetic phase persists to 12 K, with Curie-Weiss behavior above T_C. Landau fan diagrams reveal a cascade of symmetry-broken states at integer fillings ν = −4 to +4, including a ν = ±1 Chern insulator (σxy = e²/h, ρxx < 30 Ω). Comparison with Hartree-Fock theory confirms that exchange interactions in the flat band drive ferromagnetism without the need for moiré periodicity, establishing ABC-TLG as a minimal model system for flat-band magnetism.

## 1. Introduction

The discovery of superconductivity and correlated insulating states in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene established moiré flat bands as a platform for strongly correlated electron physics [1]. The requirement of precise twist angle control (1.1° ± 0.1°) remains a significant experimental challenge. ABC-stacked trilayer graphene (ABC-TLG) offers an alternative route to flat bands without fine-tuned twist angles: the intrinsic band structure of ABC-TLG hosts cubic band touching points at K and K', which become exponentially flat when a symmetry-breaking substrate potential opens a gap [2].

The density of states diverges at the van Hove singularity (VHS) near the flat band edge, making the system susceptible to ferromagnetic instability via the Stoner criterion ($U \cdot D(E_F) > 1$, where $D(E_F)$ is the VHS density of states and $U$ the Hubbard interaction strength) [3]. Exchange interaction in the flat band can drive spontaneous time-reversal symmetry breaking and anomalous Hall response even without explicit magnetic order or moiré periodicity [4]. Establishing this in a simple bilayer system would confirm that flat-band correlations are a general phenomenon independent of moiré engineering.

## 2. Device Fabrication and Measurement

ABC-TLG identified by reflection contrast spectroscopy and confirmed by transport (distinct Landau fan from ABA stacking). Encapsulated in hBN (top: 35 nm, bottom: 45 nm) with graphite top and bottom gates. Hall bar geometry (L=6 µm, W=1 µm), patterned by CHF₃/O₂ reactive ion etching. Measured in dilution refrigerator (20 mK base, 14T magnet).

## 3. Results

**Anomalous Hall ferromagnetism:**
- σAHE ≈ 0.8 e²/h at B=0, T=20 mK, carrier density n = −1.3 × 10¹¹ cm⁻²
- Square hysteresis loop (coercive field: ±18 mT)
- Ferromagnetic phase density range: ±2 × 10¹¹ cm⁻² around VHS
- T_C = 12 ± 1 K (from Curie-Weiss fit)

**Chern insulator at ν = ±1:**
- σxy = e²/h with ρxx < 30 Ω (approaching quantization)
- Robust from B = 0 to B = 3T (then enters conventional quantum Hall regime)

**Symmetry-broken cascade (ν = −4 to +4):**
All integer fillings show resurgent resistance peaks consistent with sequential spontaneous symmetry breaking (spin and valley) as predicted by HF theory.

## 4. Discussion

The observation of ferromagnetism and a near-quantized anomalous Hall effect in ABC-TLG/hBN, without any moiré potential, confirms the minimal requirements for flat-band magnetism: a flat or nearly-flat band near a VHS, combined with sufficiently strong exchange interaction ($U \approx 20–30$ meV from HF fit). This establishes ABC-TLG as a model system for understanding the interplay of topology and correlations, with experimental control parameters (carrier density, displacement field, substrate gap) that are more accessible than twist-angle control.

---

## 5. Conclusion

ABC-TLG/hBN hosts spontaneous flat-band ferromagnetism and near-quantized anomalous Hall effect at non-magic-angle conditions, establishing a moiré-free platform for strongly correlated flat-band physics with direct topological signatures.
