## Abstract

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems ground language model outputs in retrieved documents, yet hallucinations persist. Existing hallucination benchmarks do not distinguish between hallucinations that arise from retrieval failure versus generation failure, nor do they capture the severity or propagation of errors. We introduce **HalluciRank**, a fine-grained taxonomy of 7 hallucination subtypes (Context Contradiction, Retrieval Mismatch, Confabulation, Attribution Error, Temporal Conflation, Entity Substitution, and Numeric Distortion) and a benchmark of 4,200 annotated RAG outputs across 6 knowledge domains. Human annotation (κ = 0.74 inter-annotator agreement) validated 3,847 examples across the taxonomy. We evaluate 9 LLMs (GPT-4o, Claude-3.5-Sonnet, Llama-3-70B, Mistral-7B, Gemma-9B, Falcon-7B, Phi-3-medium, Qwen2-7B, Command-R+) on HalluciRank. Closed-source models outperform open-source at the Confabulation and Context Contradiction subtypes, but all models show similar vulnerability to Temporal Conflation and Numeric Distortion. HalluciRank is publicly released to enable systematic hallucination research.

## 1. Introduction

RAG systems were introduced to reduce hallucination by grounding generation in retrieved context [1]. However, empirical evaluations consistently show that RAG does not eliminate hallucination — it shifts its character. LLMs in RAG settings hallucinate in new ways: ignoring retrieved evidence (confabulation), misattributing claims to incorrect retrieved documents (attribution error), or generating plausible-but-incorrect details not present in retrieved context (context contradiction) [2].

Current hallucination benchmarks (TruthfulQA [3], FELM [4], HaluEval [5]) treat hallucination as a binary property (hallucinated/not) and do not account for the retrieval component of RAG systems. This blunt classification obscures important distinctions: a model that contradicts its retrieved context is exhibiting different failure mode than one that confabulates from parametric memory when retrieval fails. These distinctions have different remediation strategies.

## 2. Taxonomy

**HalluciRank Taxonomy (7 subtypes):**

| Code | Subtype | Definition | Example |
|------|---------|------------|---------|
| CC | Context Contradiction | Output contradicts retrieved document | Doc says "founded 1922"; output says "founded 1918" |
| RM | Retrieval Mismatch | Output based on wrong retrieved doc | Retrieves paper A, answers from paper B |
| CF | Confabulation | Fabricates detail absent from any retrieved doc | Invents citation not in context |
| AE | Attribution Error | Correct fact, wrong attribution | Attributes quote to wrong author |
| TC | Temporal Conflation | Conflates events from different time periods | Treats 2019 and 2024 events as simultaneous |
| ES | Entity Substitution | Swaps semantically similar entity | Substitutes "WHO" for "CDC" |
| ND | Numeric Distortion | Numerically plausible but incorrect | Changes 71.4% to 71.8% |

---

## 3. Benchmark Construction

4,200 RAG outputs were generated by querying 9 LLMs with 700 questions across 6 domains (science, history, medicine, law, sports, technology), each with a retrieved passage set. Three annotators per example (κ = 0.74). Final: 3,847 validated examples (91.6% acceptance rate).

---

## 4. Results

**Table 1. Hallucination rate by model and subtype (% of outputs)**

| Model | CC | CF | AE | TC | ND | Overall |
|-------|----|----|----|----|-----|---------|
| GPT-4o | 8.2 | 4.1 | 6.8 | 19.3 | 22.1 | 12.1 |
| Claude-3.5-Sonnet | 7.9 | 3.8 | 5.9 | 18.7 | 21.4 | 11.5 |
| Llama-3-70B | 14.3 | 9.2 | 11.7 | 22.4 | 28.9 | 17.3 |
| Mistral-7B | 21.4 | 18.3 | 19.2 | 28.7 | 35.2 | 24.6 |
| Phi-3-medium | 18.9 | 15.1 | 16.4 | 26.1 | 32.8 | 21.9 |

Key finding: Temporal Conflation and Numeric Distortion affect all models nearly equally (19–35%), while Confabulation shows the largest gap between closed/open source (3.8–4.1% vs. 9.2–18.3%).

---

## 5. Conclusion

HalluciRank reveals that hallucination in RAG is heterogeneous: remediation strategies must target specific subtypes. Temporal Conflation and Numeric Distortion represent universal weaknesses warranting dedicated mitigation research.
